Day 1
The first day was spent driving to California from Arizona. It took about 5 hours. The first thing we did was go to a shopping center. The main reason for this first stop was that my girlfriend wanted to visit the Hello Kitty Cafe. We did some souvenir shopping and also took advantage of the shops to do Christmas shopping. After all of this, we checked into the hotel and slept for a couple of hours. The last hours of the day were spent finding a place to eat for dinner and dessert.
Day 2
For the second day, we slept in until around 11am. We packed our bags and headed straight for Disney land. When we first got there, we went looking for a place to eat breakfast. We chose to eat at an Italian food. One thing we learned is that everything at Disney is ridiculously expensive. Next time we want to make sure we eat BEFORE getting here. The first few hours were spent at the bottom park. After 12 we switched over to the top park. At around 9pm was supposed to be the Christmas fireworks. They were cancelled unfortunately due to "high winds". Upon hearing this, my girlfriend and I, along with practically everyone else in the park, decided to head out and leave since we all came to watch the fireworks.
Day 3
On our final day, we checked out of the hotel and headed to a restaurant to eat breakfast. After eating, we headed straight to 6Flags. In all honesty, I was dreading 6Flags. I have a fear of heights and never did I dream of going. We made it after a couple of hours driving. The entire time I was nervous, not wanting to go on any rides. The first ride that my girlfriend picked out was the most extreme out of all of them in my opinion. It was called the X2. I chickened out and my girlfriend sat in the coaster by herself. I kept looking at her and I noticed how sad she got since she was doing it alone. I decided to conquer my fear and get on the ride with her. Not going to lie, it was so so so scary. But I was very happy that I went through with it in the end. After going to the most extreme ride, I felt like I was ready to conquer the rest. We stayed at the park for many many hours. I can't even remember at what time we left. It was finally time to head back home.
